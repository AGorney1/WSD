Polar bears are a large bear with an adule male weighing in at around 770 to 1500 pounds and females weighing about half of that. They have strong legs and flattened feet with webbing to help walk on ice and swimming. Polar bears are very strong swimmers and have seen swimming hundreds of miles from land. They are the largest carnivorous bear, rivaled only by the Kodiak brown bear, that lives mostly within the Arctic Circle.

Interesting Facts

  • Polar bears will roll in the snow to clean their fur.
  • One ringed seal provides enough energy for a polar bear for 11 days.
  • A group of polar bears are called either a pack or a sleuth.
  • The polar bear feed mostly on the fat of seals and the rest is left for other animals.
